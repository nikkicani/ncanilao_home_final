Research Shopping Cart software You Could Incorporate In Your Website

The 6 best eCommerce website building platforms for online stores in 2024

Shopify:
It is easy to use and offers tools you may need. It is popular with most businesses and has the best support. Shopify offers clearly labeled buttons, "do what you think they should" buttons and help documents to help someone get started for an e-commerce website. Shopify also offers designing of how you want your website to look, adding products, creating discounts, configuring payment options, and checking sale numbers.
$39 A month for unlimited products.
2.9% + $.30 per transaction with the built-in payment gateway

Square:
Integrates with Square's POS system. Weebly's site-builder holds. Downside is that Square offers limited theme and customization options compared to other tools. Square offers the beset onboarding. When someone signs up, they are prompted with a questionnaire about the business and the needs of this business/ From there it will walk you through the process of configuring up to standard.
Free for unlimited products and a Square branded site; from $29 a month to remove branding.
2.9% + $.30 per transaction

Ecwid:
Free plan is solid and enough for very small business for start ups. Paid plans are affordable and cover most needs.
Upon signing up with Ecwid, the site gives you the option to integrate your store with your existing website. Supports most websites like: Wordpress, Drupal, or company.site domain name. The "Welcome" dashboard prompts the designer with a to-do list that walks the designer through the set up of the site. The website makes you add your first products, localizing your store and choosing how you want to deliver your goods. Takes less than 15 minutes!
Free for standard features. Starting at $19 a month of professional features.
Depends on payment gateway
